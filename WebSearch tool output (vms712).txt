## Overview

Kinneret is a strategic project led by the Division for Government Medical Centers in the Ministry of Health, which aims to accelerate the initiatives and possibilities of research and cooperation. Kineret is an innovative digital project that serves as a strategic breakthrough by and for the Israeli network of government medical centers.

## Purpose and Objectives

In 2021, the Directorate of Government Medical Centers at the Israeli Ministry of Health launched the Kineret initiative to standardize clinical data across its network of public medical centers and facilitate its secondary use for research and innovation. The primary goals were to streamline data extraction, cleaning, and sharing processes, thereby enabling efficient reuse of clinical data for translational and collaborative research.

## Data and Infrastructure

The catalog offers health datasets, collected from Israel's government medical centers. The Kineret project has implemented solutions using uniform extraction processes, while optimizing information quality, anonymizing the information, and using the international OMOP research standard.

## Coverage

The Directorate oversees a national network of 25 government healthcare institutions, including 11 general medical centers, 9 mental health centers, and 5 geriatric care facilities.

The website serves as a portal for researchers and institutions seeking access to this standardized healthcare data infrastructure.
